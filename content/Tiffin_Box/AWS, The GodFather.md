Amazon Web Services (AWS) is the world's dominant cloud platform. It holds roughly **a third of the entire cloud market**, serves millions of customers across every industry, and generates more profit than Amazon's retail business. But its origin story is stranger,

>[!tip] Origin story
> AWS wasn't a grand vision from day one. Amazon built it to solve their own scaling problems — then realized it was a product. [Andy Jassy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andy_Jassy), who led AWS, later became CEO of Amazon. AWS today makes more profit than Amazon's entire retail business.

### The Origin

In the early 2000s, Amazon's engineering teams had a problem: every new project meant waiting weeks for servers to arrive and be configured. Growth was fast; infrastructure was the bottleneck.

A small group of engineers built a solution — standardized, programmable infrastructure accessible via API. It worked so well internally that someone asked: _what if we sold this to everyone?_

On March 14, 2006, AWS launched publicly with just two services:

- **S3** — store files in the cloud
- **EC2** — rent virtual servers by the hour

Ten cents per gigabyte. No upfront commitment. No minimum term.

### Where It Sits Today

- ~32% global cloud market share — the largest of any provider
- 30+ geographic regions worldwide
- Customers range from two-person startups to the CIA
